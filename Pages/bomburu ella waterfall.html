Bomburu Ella Waterfall

Uva-Paranagama, Uva Province,Sri lanka

Bomburu Ella Waterfall, also known as Perawella Falls, is a magnificent and scenic waterfall located in the Uva-Paranagama area of the Uva Province, Sri Lanka. It is the widest waterfall in the country, made up of several small waterfalls that converge to form this breathtaking natural wonder. The waterfall is fed by the Bomburu Ella Reservoir and is a popular destination for both locals and tourists who seek to experience its beauty and the tranquility of its surroundings. The waterfall is situated at an elevation of approximately 1500 meters above sea level, adding to its majestic appeal. Visitors can enjoy a hike through lush forests to reach the waterfall, which provides a rewarding and immersive nature experience. The journey to Bomburu Ella is often as enchanting as the waterfall itself, with diverse flora and fauna along the way. Bomburu Ella is not only a place of natural beauty but also holds cultural and ecological significance. The surrounding area is rich in biodiversity and offers opportunities for eco-tourism and nature conservation efforts. The pristine environment and the sound of cascading water create a serene atmosphere, making it an ideal spot for picnics, photography, and relaxation. Despite its popularity, Bomburu Ella remains relatively untouched by commercial development, preserving its natural charm and serenity. The best time to visit Bomburu Ella is during the rainy season, when the waterfall is at its fullest and most spectacular. However, the trail can be slippery and challenging, so visitors are advised to wear appropriate footwear and take necessary precautions.
Whether you are an avid nature lover, a photography enthusiast, or simply seeking a peaceful retreat, Bomburu Ella Waterfall offers a captivating experience that showcases the raw beauty of Sri Lanka's natural landscapes.
