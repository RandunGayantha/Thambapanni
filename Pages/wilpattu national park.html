Wilpattu National Park

Puttalam, North Western Province, Sri lanka

Wilpattu National Park is one of the oldest and largest national parks in Sri Lanka, located in the North Western Province. It spans an impressive area of approximately 1,317 square kilometers, consisting of dense scrub jungle, lakes, and open grassy plains. The park is renowned for its diverse wildlife, including a significant population of leopards, making it one of the best places in the world to observe these elusive big cats. Other wildlife species found here include elephants, sloth bears, water buffalo, and a variety of bird species. Wilpattu is characterized by its unique landscape dotted with "villus" (natural lakes), which attract wildlife during the dry season. These villus are vital for the survival of many species and provide excellent opportunities for wildlife viewing. Visitors to Wilpattu can enjoy guided safaris that traverse through its rugged terrain, offering glimpses of its rich biodiversity and pristine natural beauty. The park's remote location and serene ambiance make it a preferred destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.
Protecting a wide range of habitats and species, Wilpattu National Park plays a crucial role in conservation efforts in Sri Lanka, ensuring the preservation of its natural heritage for future generations to appreciate and enjoy.
