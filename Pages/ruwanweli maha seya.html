Ruwanweli Maha Seya

Anuradhapura, North Central Province,Sri lanka

Ruwanweli Maha Seya, also known as the Great Stupa, is one of the most important and iconic stupas in Sri Lanka, located in the sacred city of Anuradhapura. It was built by King Dutugemunu in 140 B.C. The stupa has a diameter of 90 meters at the base and a height of 103 meters, making it one of the tallest ancient monuments in the world. The structure is surrounded by an elephant wall consisting of 344 elephant statues. Ruwanweli Maha Seya is a symbol of Buddhist heritage and is a site of pilgrimage and reverence for Buddhists around the world.
The stupa was built to enshrine relics of the Buddha, and it is believed that some of the most sacred relics, including the Buddha's collarbone, are housed here. The construction of Ruwanweli Maha Seya marked a significant achievement in ancient Sri Lankan engineering and architecture, showcasing the advanced capabilities of the time.The site itself is part of the larger Anuradhapura historical complex, which includes several other stupas, monasteries, and ancient structures. Anuradhapura was one of the ancient capitals of Sri Lanka, renowned for its well-preserved ruins of ancient Sri Lankan civilization. The city's significance as a center of Buddhism is reflected in the numerous temples and stupas that can be found there.
Ruwanweli Maha Seya is particularly notable for its architectural beauty. The stupa is adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures, including the famous elephant wall. This wall, which encircles the base of the stupa, is decorated with life-sized statues of elephants, symbolizing strength and stability.Throughout its history, Ruwanweli Maha Seya has undergone several restorations. The most significant restoration efforts took place in the 19th and 20th centuries, ensuring that the stupa remains a vibrant and active place of worship. Today, it continues to be a focal point for Buddhist festivals and ceremonies, drawing devotees from all over the world.The Great Stupa's surroundings are serene and peaceful, providing a reflective atmosphere for meditation and worship. Visitors to Ruwanweli Maha Seya can explore the site and experience the profound sense of spirituality that permeates the area.
