Nil Diya Pokuna

Aradhana Gala, Anuradhapura, Sri lanka

Nil Diya Pokuna, meaning "Blue Water Pond," is a historic site located in Aradhana Gala, Anuradhapura, Sri Lanka. It is renowned for its unique ancient bathing pool carved out of rock, which dates back to the Anuradhapura period (3rd century BC to 10th century AD). The main attraction of Nil Diya Pokuna is its perfectly preserved twin ponds, carved side by side into the natural rock formation. The ponds are interconnected by a small channel through which water flows, believed to have been ingeniously designed to maintain water purity and provide a tranquil bathing experience for ancient monks. The architectural marvel of Nil Diya Pokuna showcases the advanced engineering skills and artistic craftsmanship of ancient Sri Lankans. The site is surrounded by serene natural surroundings, creating a peaceful ambiance that invites visitors to reflect on its historical significance and cultural heritage. Nil Diya Pokuna remains a testament to Sri Lanka's rich history and Buddhist heritage, offering visitors a glimpse into the sophisticated architectural and hydraulic engineering techniques of the Anuradhapura era. Visiting Nil Diya Pokuna provides a unique opportunity to explore an ancient marvel and appreciate its cultural and historical value amidst the serene landscapes of Anuradhapura.
